ObsCalc India — Free Obstetric Calculator Suite

This free obstetric calculator suite provides Indian obstetricians and gynaecologists with all the clinical tools needed at the bedside. All calculators follow FOGSI, ACOG, RCOG and WHO guidelines adapted for Indian clinical practice.

Gestational Age Calculators

GA by LMP: Uses Naegele's Rule — EDD = LMP + 280 days, adjusted for cycle length. Shows current GA, trimester, and EDD.

GA by Scan: Back-calculates EDD from ultrasound GA. Most accurate method in the first trimester using CRL. Enter the scan date and GA as reported by the sonologist.

GA on Given Date: Calculates the exact gestational age on any specified date — useful for documenting GA at admission, operation note, or discharge summary.

LMP from EDD: Reverse calculation — enter a known EDD to find the equivalent LMP and approximate conception date.

Expected Fetal Weight — Hadlock Formula

The Hadlock 1985 formula is the standard EFW formula used in Indian obstetric practice. This calculator uses the 4-parameter formula (BPD+HC+AC+FL) when HC is available, and the 3-parameter (BPD+AC+FL) otherwise. Results include ±15% variability range and an approximate percentile classification (SGA/AGA/LGA) based on gestational age.

Apgar Score

Calculates Apgar score from the 5 criteria — Appearance, Pulse, Grimace, Activity and Respiration. Each scored 0–2. Total of 7–10 is normal; 4–6 requires intervention; 0–3 requires immediate resuscitation.

Bishop Score for Induction of Labour

The Bishop score assesses cervical favourability before induction of labour. Scores all 5 parameters — dilation, effacement, station, consistency and position. Score ≥9 indicates a highly favourable cervix suitable for direct oxytocin induction. Score <6 requires cervical ripening.

VBAC Eligibility

8-point checklist for Trial of Labour After Caesarean (TOLAC/VBAC) per FOGSI and ACOG guidelines. Covers scar type, number of previous CS, presentation, fetal weight, scar thickness, interpregnancy interval, previous vaginal delivery and CS indication.

PPH Risk Assessment

15 weighted risk factors for postpartum haemorrhage giving Low/Moderate/High/Very High risk classification with specific management recommendations per RCOG and FOGSI guidelines.

GDM Screening — DIPSI Protocol

DIPSI (Diabetes in Pregnancy Study Group India) recommends a 75g oral glucose challenge in the non-fasting state. Blood glucose ≥140 mg/dL at 2 hours is diagnostic. FOGSI recommends universal screening of all Indian pregnant women at first ANC visit and again at 24–28 weeks. High-risk women receive an additional screen at 32–34 weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is gestational age calculated from LMP in India?

Using Naegele's Rule — add 280 days to the first day of the LMP assuming a 28-day cycle. For other cycle lengths, the difference from 28 days is added or subtracted. This gives the estimated due date (EDD).

Which Hadlock formula is used for EFW in India?

The Hadlock 1985 formula is standard in Indian practice. The 4-parameter formula (BPD+HC+AC+FL) is preferred when all measurements are available. Results have ±15% variability and should be interpreted alongside clinical findings.

What is a good Bishop score for induction of labour?

Score ≥9 is highly favourable — direct oxytocin induction appropriate. Score 6–8 is favourable with reasonable success. Score <6 requires cervical ripening with prostaglandins or Foley balloon before oxytocin.

What is the DIPSI protocol for GDM in India?

75g oral glucose (non-fasting). Blood glucose at 2 hours ≥140 mg/dL = GDM. FOGSI recommends universal screening at first ANC visit and at 24–28 weeks for all Indian women.

What are the VBAC criteria per FOGSI?

Single previous low transverse LSCS, cephalic presentation, scar thickness ≥3.5mm, no recurring indication, interpregnancy interval ≥18 months. Must be conducted in a facility with immediate access to emergency CS and blood transfusion.

What is a normal Apgar score?

7–10 is normal — routine newborn care. 4–6 is moderately depressed — stimulation and supplemental oxygen. 0–3 is severely depressed — immediate resuscitation including positive pressure ventilation.
