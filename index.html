Why These Calculators Use Indian Guidelines

Many standard medical calculators use Western or WHO cutoffs which are not always appropriate for Indians. For example, Indians develop diabetes and cardiovascular disease at lower BMI values, so Indian guidelines define overweight as BMI ≥ 23 rather than ≥ 25. Similarly, the rabies PEP schedule in India follows the 4-dose Essen regimen (Days 0, 3, 7, 28) which differs from some international schedules. These tools are built specifically to reflect Indian clinical practice.

About These Tools

All tools on this site are built and maintained by a practising doctor in India. They are intended for clinical use by healthcare professionals and for patient education. No data is collected or stored on any server — all calculations happen in your browser. Vaccination records in the UIP tracker are saved only on your own device.
