UIP Vaccine Schedule India — Complete Guide

India's Universal Immunisation Programme (UIP) is one of the largest public health programmes in the world. It provides free vaccines to all children from birth through 16 years of age. This tracker automatically calculates all vaccine due dates from your child's date of birth.

UIP Vaccines at Each Age

At Birth: BCG, OPV-0 (Zero Dose), Hepatitis B-0

6 Weeks: OPV-1, DPT-1, Hepatitis B-2, IPV-1, Hib-1, PCV-1, RVV-1

10 Weeks: OPV-2, DPT-2, Hib-2, PCV-2, RVV-2

14 Weeks: OPV-3, DPT-3, Hepatitis B-3, IPV-2, Hib-3, PCV-3, RVV-3

9 Months: MR-1 (Measles-Rubella), OPV Booster, JE-1 (in endemic areas), Vitamin A-1

12 Months: Hepatitis A-1, Typhoid conjugate vaccine (TCV)

15–18 Months: MMR / MR-2, DPT Booster-1, OPV Booster, Hib Booster, PCV Booster, Varicella-1, Hepatitis A-2, JE-2

2 Years: Typhoid booster, Vitamin A (every 6 months till 5 years)

4–6 Years: DPT Booster-2, OPV Booster, Varicella-2, MMR Booster

10 Years: Td (Tetanus-diphtheria)

16 Years: Td booster

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the UIP vaccine schedule in India?

The Universal Immunisation Programme (UIP) provides free vaccines from birth to 16 years including BCG, OPV, DPT, Hepatitis B, IPV, Hib, PCV, Rotavirus, MR, JE, and Td vaccines at specified age intervals.

Which vaccines are given at birth in India?

Three vaccines are given at birth under UIP: BCG (for tuberculosis), OPV-0 (zero dose oral polio), and Hepatitis B birth dose (HepB-0). These should be given within 24 hours of birth ideally.

When is the MMR vaccine given in India?

Under UIP India, MR (Measles-Rubella) vaccine is given at 9 months and again at 15–18 months. MMR (which also covers Mumps) is part of the IAP recommended private schedule given at 12 months and 15–18 months.

Are UIP vaccines free in India?

Yes. All UIP vaccines are provided completely free at government health centres, PHCs, CHCs, and Anganwadi centres across India.

What is the difference between UIP and IAP vaccine schedule?

UIP is the government-mandated free schedule. IAP (Indian Academy of Pediatrics) recommends additional vaccines like Hepatitis A, Varicella (chickenpox), Typhoid conjugate, and MMR which may be available at private clinics. This tracker includes both.
