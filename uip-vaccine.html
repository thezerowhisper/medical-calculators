🔄 Catch-Up Vaccination Schedule for Missed Doses

If doses were missed, do not restart the series — continue from where it was left off. Key rule: maintain minimum intervals between doses.

Vaccine Catch-Up Age Limit Minimum Interval Between Doses Notes
BCG Up to 1 year AGE LIMIT Single dose only Not recommended after 1 year of age for routine catch-up
OPV Up to 5 years FLEXIBLE 4 weeks Complete missed primary doses; IPV preferred if ≥1 year
Rotavirus Last dose before 8 months STRICT LIMIT 4 weeks ⚠️ Do NOT give after 8 months. Safety cutoff due to intussusception risk
DPT / Pentavalent Primary series up to 7 years FLEXIBLE 4 weeks between primary doses After 7 years use Td instead of DPT. Min 6 months between last primary and booster

Golden Rule: Never restart a vaccine series from scratch — always continue from where it left off. A dose given late is still effective. Maintain minimum intervals; longer gaps do not reduce immunity.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions
What is the catch-up vaccination schedule for missed UIP doses in India?+
As per IAP 2024 guidelines, missed UIP vaccines should be given at the earliest opportunity. The key rule: never restart a series — continue from where it was left off. For DPT/Pentavalent, maintain a minimum 4-week interval. BCG catch-up is up to 1 year. Rotavirus must be completed before 8 months. PCV dosing depends on age at first dose. MR/MMR catch-up can be given up to 5 years. Longer gaps between doses do not reduce vaccine effectiveness.
What is the BCG vaccine catch-up age limit in India?+
BCG can be given as a catch-up dose up to 1 year of age. After 1 year, routine BCG catch-up is not recommended under UIP or IAP guidelines. The birth dose should ideally be given within 24 hours of birth.
What is the Rotavirus vaccine upper age limit? Can it be given after 8 months?+
No. The Rotavirus vaccine must not be given after 8 months of age. This is a strict safety cutoff established by WHO and IAP due to the risk of intussusception (a type of bowel obstruction) in older infants. If the child is older than 8 months and has missed the Rotavirus vaccine, skip it entirely — do not give it late.
What is the DPT booster schedule under India UIP?+
DPT boosters under India UIP: Booster 1 at 16–24 months, Booster 2 at 5–6 years. Then TD (not DPT) at 10 years and 16 years. The primary series (as Pentavalent) is at 6, 10, 14 weeks. Minimum interval between last primary dose and first booster is 6 months.
What is the PCV catch-up schedule in India?+
PCV catch-up depends on age at first dose: Starting at 2–6 months: 3 primary doses + 1 booster at 12–15 months. Starting at 7–11 months: 2 doses + 1 booster. Starting at 12–23 months: 2 doses, no booster. Starting at 24 months or older: 1 dose only. Minimum interval between doses: 8 weeks.
What vaccines are given at birth under India UIP?+
Three vaccines are given at birth under India UIP: BCG (for TB protection), OPV-0 (Oral Polio Vaccine birth dose), and HepB-0 (Hepatitis B birth dose). All three should be given within 24 hours of birth, or as early as possible before hospital discharge.
What is the UIP vaccination schedule at birth, 6 weeks, 10 weeks, and 14 weeks?+
Birth: BCG, OPV-0, HepB-0. At 6 weeks: OPV-1, Pentavalent-1 (DPT+HepB+Hib), IPV-1, Rotavirus-1, PCV-1. At 10 weeks: OPV-2, Pentavalent-2, IPV-2, Rotavirus-2, PCV-2. At 14 weeks: OPV-3, Pentavalent-3, IPV-3, Rotavirus-3, PCV-3.
Can vaccines be given if the child has a mild fever or cold?+
Yes. Mild fever (below 38.5°C), mild cold, cough, or mild diarrhoea are NOT contraindications to vaccination per IAP and UIP guidelines. Only severe febrile illness (fever above 38.5°C) warrants a brief delay. Never miss a vaccination opportunity for minor illness.
What is the MR vaccine catch-up schedule in India?+
MR (Measles-Rubella) vaccine is given at 9 months and 16–24 months under UIP India. If missed, catch-up can be given at any age up to 5 years. Second dose should be at least 4 weeks after the first. Having measles disease previously does not replace the need for the MR vaccine — still vaccinate per schedule for rubella protection.
What is the TD vaccine schedule for school children in India?+
TD (Tetanus-Diphtheria) is given at 10 years and 16 years under India UIP — these are school-age boosters. Catch-up: the 10-year dose can be given up to 12 years; the 16-year dose up to 18 years. TD does not contain pertussis (unlike DPT), as pertussis boosters cause more side effects in older children and adults.
